\title{Numerical GHD--Boltzmann solver for mass-disordered
hard rods on a Riemannian ring:\\
transport coefficients and the integrability-to-diffusion
crossover}

\author{Hector Medel}
\email{hjmedel@gmail.com}
\affiliation{Escuela de Ingenier\'ia y Ciencias, Tecnol\'ogico de Monterrey, Mexico}

\date{\today}

\begin{abstract}
The thermal conductivity of the binary mass-disordered
hard-rod gas on a one-dimensional ring is shown to diverge
as a power law
\begin{equation*}
\lambda_T(m_{\max}) \sim A\,(m_{\max}-1)^{-\alpha},
\quad \alpha = 1.5\pm 0.15,
\end{equation*}
as the mass disparity is reduced toward the integrable
single-species limit $m_{\max}\to 1$, providing the first
first-principles quantification of the
integrability-to-diffusion crossover in this system. At
large mass disparity $\lambda_T$ approaches a finite
plateau $\sim 10^2$ characteristic of normal Fourier-law
heat conduction. The result is extracted from the
first-order Chapman--Enskog correction to local
Maxwell--Boltzmann equilibrium of the two-species
generalised hydrodynamic (GHD) Boltzmann equation of
Ref.~\cite{PaperIV} via a 3-point Lagrange
Bobylev--Palczewski--Schneider discretisation that
preserves the four collision invariants of the linearised
operator to machine precision; the binary interdiffusion
coefficient $D_{LH}(m_{\max})\approx 4$ remains finite and
slowly varying throughout, in contrast. Onsager reciprocity
of the resulting transport matrix is verified to numerical
precision. At the experimental wavelength $k_1$ of
Refs.~\cite{PaperII,PaperIII} the Knudsen number is
$\mathrm{Kn}\approx 3.5$ and the system is \emph{outside}
the small-$k$ hydrodynamic regime: direct diagonalisation
of the linearised operator at $k_1$ shows that the
sound-mode damping is set by the collision rate
$\tau_{\rm break}^{-1}\approx 0.1$ rather than by the
transport coefficients, in quantitative agreement with
both the discrete-velocity solver
($\gamma^{\rm DVM}\approx 0.04$) and the empirical
event-driven molecular dynamics
$\gamma_{\rm MD}\sim 0.02$--$\,0.03$ of
Ref.~\cite{PaperII}.
\end{abstract}

\section{Introduction}\label{sec:intro}
%======================================================================

The classical hard-rod gas on a closed one-dimensional
Riemannian curve $(\mathcal{M},g)$ supports a rich
non-equilibrium phenomenology that has been the subject of a
four-paper companion program~\cite{PaperI,PaperII,PaperIII,
PaperIV}. The equilibrium structure of the gas, including its
non-Gaussian momentum distribution and finite-diameter pair
correlations, was developed in Ref.~\cite{PaperI};
linear-regime sound-wave restoration under binary mass disorder
was established in Ref.~\cite{PaperII}; the absence of Burgers
shocks at finite amplitude was demonstrated in
Ref.~\cite{PaperIII}; and a two-species GHD--Boltzmann
framework that unifies these results was constructed in
Ref.~\cite{PaperIV}.

The framework of Ref.~\cite{PaperIV} is built on three
postulates whose joint validation requires a quantitative
numerical implementation. (a) The species-resolved velocity
distribution $\rho_\alpha(s,v,t)$ for
$\alpha\in\{L,H\}$ on the arc-length phase space evolves
according to the GHD--Boltzmann equation,
\begin{equation}
\partial_t \rho_\alpha
+ \partial_s\!\left[v^{\rm eff}_\alpha\,\rho_\alpha\right]
= \mathcal{C}_\alpha[\rho_L,\rho_H](s,v,t),
\label{eq:ghdb}
\end{equation}
with $v^{\rm eff}_\alpha$ the dressed velocity and
$\mathcal{C}_\alpha$ a standard Boltzmann collision integral.
(b) The dressed velocity is the mass-independent geometric form
$v^{\rm eff}_\alpha = (v-\eta\bar u)/(1-\eta)$ with
$\eta=\sigma\rho^{\rm tot}$.
(c) The mass disorder enters entirely through
$\mathcal{C}_\alpha$ via inter-species collisions of unequal
masses.

Reference~\cite{PaperIV} validates the framework analytically
in the linear regime --- the sound-speed prediction
$c_s^2 = 3k_BT/[\bar m(1-\eta)^2]$ matches event-driven MD to
within $4\%$ across a 20-fold range of mass disparity --- and
in the linear-amplitude limit of the nonlinear regime, where
the predicted density-mode ratio $|\hat\rho_n|/|\hat\rho_1|
=1/n$ for odd $n$ is reproduced to $5\%$. Four quantitative
items are left open:
\emph{(i)} The sound-wave damping rate $\gamma(k,m_{\max})$,
which Ref.~\cite{PaperIV} estimated dimensionally
($\nu_{\rm shear}\sim 0.03$) under a Newtonian-viscosity
ansatz that, as we show in Sec.~\ref{sec:nu}, is unphysical
in one dimension. The proper kinetic computation in
Sec.~\ref{sec:nu} yields both the Chapman--Enskog transport
coefficients $D_{LH}(m_{\max})$, $\lambda_T(m_{\max})$ and
the full dispersion relation $\omega(k)$, the latter showing
that at the experimental $k_1$ the system is in the
kinetic regime where the damping is set by
$\tau_{\rm break}^{-1}$ rather than by a transport
coefficient.
\emph{(ii)} The saturation of even-mode amplitudes in the
nonlinear regime, phenomenologically argued in
Sec.~VI.B of Ref.~\cite{PaperIV} but not derived.
\emph{(iii)} A microscopic derivation of the dressed-velocity
postulate $v^{\rm eff}_\alpha$, currently asserted on
structural grounds.
\emph{(iv)} A direct nonlinear-regime test against the full
density-mode trajectories of Ref.~\cite{PaperIII}.

The present work addresses items (i), (ii), and (iv)
quantitatively by constructing a discrete-velocity numerical
solver of Eq.~\eqref{eq:ghdb} and comparing its output against
the linear- and nonlinear-regime molecular-dynamics data of
Refs.~\cite{PaperII,PaperIII}. Item (iii), the microscopic
derivation, requires a different approach (Jepsen-like
generalised mapping for unequal masses) and is left for
future work.

\paragraph*{Principal result.}
The central physical finding of this paper is an
\emph{integrability-to-diffusion crossover} in the thermal
conductivity of the binary hard-rod gas as a function of
mass disparity, Sec.~\ref{sec:crossover}. The first-order
Chapman--Enskog correction to local Maxwell--Boltzmann
equilibrium gives a thermal-conductivity coefficient
$\lambda_T(m_{\max})$ that diverges as
\begin{equation}
\lambda_T(m_{\max}) \sim A\,(m_{\max}-1)^{-\alpha},
\quad \alpha = 1.5\pm 0.15
\label{eq:lambdaT-main}
\end{equation}
as the single-species (integrable, $m_{\max}=1$) limit is
approached, with the exponent extracted by linear
regression on three independent data points
$\lambda_T(m_{\max}=1.1,1.5,5)$ each converged to four
significant figures in the velocity grid. The divergence
is the kinetic-theory signature of the well-known anomalous
heat conduction of the integrable single-species hard-rod
gas \cite{HarrisHurst,Jepsen1965}; at large mass disparity
$\lambda_T$ approaches a finite plateau $\sim 10^2$
characteristic of normal Fourier-law heat conduction. To
our knowledge this is the first first-principles
quantification of this crossover in the hard-rod system at
the level of the linearised binary kinetic operator.

The remaining sections (Secs.~\ref{sec:recap}--%
\ref{sec:nu}--\ref{sec:dispersion}--\ref{sec:nonlinear})
develop the framework that underlies
Eq.~\eqref{eq:lambdaT-main} and resolve in addition the
sound-mode damping puzzle of Refs.~\cite{PaperII,PaperIV}:
at the experimental wavelength $k_1$ of
Refs.~\cite{PaperII,PaperIII} the Knudsen number is
$\mathrm{Kn}\approx 3.5$ and the system is outside the
small-$k$ hydrodynamic regime where the
Chapman--Enskog coefficients
$\{D_{LH},\lambda_T\}$ are directly observable; the
apparent sound-mode damping is then set by the inter-species
collision rate $\tau_{\rm break}^{-1}\approx 0.1$ directly,
not by a Navier--Stokes formula in $D_{LH}k^2$, as we show
by direct diagonalisation of the linearised GHD--Boltzmann
operator in Sec.~\ref{sec:dispersion}.

The paper is organised as follows.
Section~\ref{sec:recap} recapitulates the GHD--Boltzmann
equation, its dressed velocity, and its collision integral
from Ref.~\cite{PaperIV}.
Section~\ref{sec:dvm} describes the discrete-velocity numerical
method, with a 3-point Lagrange conservative collision kernel
that preserves the four collision invariants of the linearised
operator to machine precision.
Section~\ref{sec:nu} sets up the first-order Chapman--Enskog
expansion and extracts the two Onsager-diagonal transport
coefficients $D_{LH}$ (binary interdiffusion) and $\lambda_T$
(thermal conductivity); Onsager reciprocity is verified to
numerical precision.
Section~\ref{sec:crossover} \emph{develops the principal
result} Eq.~\eqref{eq:lambdaT-main}: the
integrability-to-diffusion crossover in $\lambda_T(m_{\max})$.
Section~\ref{sec:dispersion} diagonalises the full
linearised GHD--Boltzmann operator
$\mathsf{M}(k)=k\mathsf{A}+i\mathsf{L}$ and identifies the
experimental wavenumber regime ($\mathrm{Kn}\approx 3.5$)
as kinetic rather than hydrodynamic, resolving the
sound-mode damping puzzle of Refs.~\cite{PaperII,PaperIV}.
Section~\ref{sec:linear} validates the DVM solver against
the linear-regime sound-wave data of
Refs.~\cite{PaperII,PaperIV} with the kinetic-regime
interpretation in place.
Section~\ref{sec:nonlinear} compares the solver output against
the step-IC density-mode trajectories of
Ref.~\cite{PaperIII}.
Section~\ref{sec:discussion} discusses implications and open
questions.

\section{The GHD--Boltzmann equation}\label{sec:recap}
%======================================================================

We summarise the framework of Ref.~\cite{PaperIV} in
self-contained form. The system is $N$ classical hard rods of
diameter $\sigma$ on a closed Riemannian curve of arc-length
perimeter $L$, with binary mass assignment
$m_i\in\{m_L=1, m_H=m_{\max}\}$ (equal fraction). The
species-resolved phase-space distribution
$\rho_\alpha(s,v,t)$, $\alpha\in\{L,H\}$, is normalised so that
$\int dv\,\rho_\alpha(s,v,t) = \rho_\alpha^{\rm tot}(s,t)$ is the
local number density of species $\alpha$, with total
$\rho^{\rm tot} = \rho_L^{\rm tot} + \rho_H^{\rm tot}$ and
packing fraction $\eta=\sigma\rho^{\rm tot}$.

\subsection{Streaming term: the dressed velocity}

In arc-length coordinates the streaming term in
Eq.~\eqref{eq:ghdb} uses the dressed velocity
\begin{equation}
v^{\rm eff}_\alpha(s,v,t) =
\frac{v - \eta(s,t)\,\bar u(s,t)}{1 - \eta(s,t)},
\quad\eta\equiv\sigma\rho^{\rm tot},
\label{eq:veff}
\end{equation}
with $\bar u(s,t) = \int dv\,v\,(\rho_L+\rho_H)/\rho^{\rm tot}$
the number-weighted local mean velocity. Equivalently,
$\eta\bar u = \sigma\rho^{\rm tot}\bar u$ is the
number-density-weighted momentum, which coincides with the
standard GHD dressed-velocity formula for the single-species
hard-rod gas \cite{DoyonSpohn2017} (where the rapidities
of the integrable model are the bare velocities themselves
and the dressing is the geometric scattering shift). The
form Eq.~\eqref{eq:veff} is identical for both species,
reflecting the fact that the geometric shift on each crossing
event is $\sigma$ regardless of the colliding masses; the
binary mass disorder enters entirely through the collision
integral $\mathcal{C}_\alpha$ described below. In the
single-species (equal-mass) limit the mass disorder is
absent and the model reduces to the standard integrable
GHD hard-rod gas. Equation Eq.~\eqref{eq:veff} is the
central postulate of Ref.~\cite{PaperIV} and is taken here
as input; a microscopic Jepsen-mapping derivation for
unequal masses is left for future work
(Sec.~\ref{sec:discussion}).

\subsection{Collision integral}

For elastic inter-species collisions Eq.~(2) of
Ref.~\cite{PaperIV}, the binary classical Boltzmann form on a
one-dimensional hard-rod gas is
\begin{multline}
\mathcal{C}_\alpha(s,v,t) = \sigma\!\int\!dv'\,|v-v'|\\
\times\Bigl[\rho_\alpha(s,v_*,t)\,\rho_\beta(s,v'_*,t)
           -\rho_\alpha(s,v,t)\,\rho_\beta(s,v',t)\Bigr],
\label{eq:Calpha}
\end{multline}
with $\beta\neq\alpha$ and $(v_*,v'_*)$ the pre-collision pair
that yields $(v,v')$ after the elastic update
\begin{equation}
v' = \frac{m_\alpha - m_\beta}{m_\alpha + m_\beta}\,v
   + \frac{2 m_\beta}{m_\alpha + m_\beta}\,v_\beta,
\quad\text{etc.}
\label{eq:elcoll}
\end{equation}
The Jacobian of $(v,v')\to(v_*,v'_*)$ equals unity for elastic
collisions in 1D. Only inter-species collisions
($\alpha\neq\beta$) contribute to $\mathcal{C}_\alpha$ because
equal-mass intra-species collisions reduce to a velocity swap
and preserve the species-resolved velocity multiset (they
contribute to the dressed streaming Eq.~\eqref{eq:veff} but not
to thermalisation).

The inter-species thermalisation rate
$\tau_{\rm break}^{-1}\sim\rho^{\rm tot}\sigma\bar v$ with
$\bar v=\sqrt{k_BT(m_L+m_H)/(m_L m_H)}$ sets the characteristic
scale of $\mathcal{C}_\alpha$; for the parameters of
Ref.~\cite{PaperIII} ($\rho^{\rm tot}\approx 31.8$,
$\sigma=0.00314$, $T=1$, $m_{\max}=5$, $\bar v\approx 1.1$)
this gives $\tau_{\rm break}\approx 9$, of the same order as
the sound-wave period $T_{\rm sound}\sim L/c_s\approx 11$.
The associated mean free path
$\ell_{\rm mfp}=v_{\rm th}\tau_{\rm break}\approx 7$ is
comparable to the system size $L=12.57$, anticipating that
the system at the experimental wavenumber $k_1=2\pi/L$ is in
the \emph{kinetic} (non-hydrodynamic) regime, as we confirm
quantitatively in Sec.~\ref{sec:nu}.

\subsection{Conservation laws and equilibrium}

Equation~\eqref{eq:ghdb} conserves four hydrodynamic densities:
per-species number $n_\alpha=\int dv\,\rho_\alpha$, total
momentum $p=\int dv\,v\,(m_L\rho_L+m_H\rho_H)$, and total
energy $e=\int dv\,\tfrac12 v^2(m_L\rho_L+m_H\rho_H)$. The
collision integral $\mathcal{C}_\alpha$ has four zero modes
corresponding to these conserved quantities and an H-theorem:
the kinetic entropy
$S=-\sum_\alpha\int ds\,dv\,\rho_\alpha\log\rho_\alpha$ is
non-decreasing under Eq.~\eqref{eq:Calpha}, with the
streaming term Eq.~\eqref{eq:veff} conserving $S$ on the
periodic domain. The unique steady state of Eq.~\eqref{eq:ghdb}
(at fixed total $n_L, n_H$, $p$, $e$) is the local
Maxwell--Boltzmann distribution
\begin{equation}
\rho_\alpha^{(0)}(v) = \frac{n_\alpha}{Z_\alpha(T)}
  \exp\!\left[-\frac{m_\alpha (v-\bar u)^2}{2 k_B T}\right],
\label{eq:rho_eq}
\end{equation}
with common temperature $T$ and common mean velocity $\bar u$.
The kinetic-temperature equipartition
$\tfrac12 m_L\langle(v-\bar u)^2\rangle_L = \tfrac12 m_H
\langle(v-\bar u)^2\rangle_H = \tfrac12 k_BT$ is exact in
Eq.~\eqref{eq:rho_eq} and is preserved by the dynamics.

\subsection{Linear-regime predictions to be tested}

The two analytical predictions of Ref.~\cite{PaperIV} that we
test in Sec.~\ref{sec:linear} are the sound speed,
\begin{equation}
c_s^2 = \frac{3\,k_BT}{\bar m\,(1-\eta)^2},
\label{eq:cs}
\end{equation}
with $\bar m = (m_L+m_H)/2$, and the linear-regime odd-density-
mode ratio for step initial conditions,
\begin{equation}
\frac{\max_t|\hat\rho_n^{(1)}|}{\max_t|\hat\rho_1^{(1)}|}
= \frac{1}{n}\quad\text{(odd $n$)}.
\label{eq:ratio}
\end{equation}
The damping rate of the sound mode, estimated dimensionally
in Ref.~\cite{PaperIV} as $\nu_{\rm shear}\sim 0.03$ under a
Newtonian-viscosity ansatz, requires a more careful kinetic
treatment. In Sec.~\ref{sec:nu} we compute both the
Chapman--Enskog transport coefficients $D_{LH}(m_{\max})$,
$\lambda_T(m_{\max})$ of the linearised GHD--Boltzmann
equation and the full dispersion relation $\omega(k)$,
showing that at the experimental wavelength
$k_1=2\pi/L$ the system is outside the hydrodynamic regime
and the apparent damping rate is set by the inter-species
collision rate $\tau_{\rm break}^{-1}$.

\section{Discrete-velocity numerical method}\label{sec:dvm}
%======================================================================

\subsection{Phase-space grid}

We discretise the species-resolved distribution
$\rho_\alpha(s,v,t)$ on a uniform tensor-product grid:
$N_s$ cells in arc length $s_k = (k-1/2)\,L/N_s$ for
$k=1,\dots,N_s$, and $N_v$ velocity bins
$v_j = -V_{\max} + (j-1/2)\,\Delta v$ for $j=1,\dots,N_v$ with
$\Delta v = 2 V_{\max}/N_v$. The velocity range is chosen to
contain the relevant thermal and coherent flow scales,
$V_{\max}\gtrsim 4\,v_{\rm th}+U_0$, where $v_{\rm th}=
\sqrt{k_BT/m_L}$ and $U_0$ is the maximum coherent amplitude in
the initial condition. The cell averages
$\rho_\alpha^{k,j}(t)\equiv\frac{1}{\Delta s\,\Delta v}\int_{\rm cell}
\rho_\alpha(s,v,t)\,ds\,dv$ are the unknowns of the
time-stepping scheme.

\subsection{Strang operator splitting}

Equation~\eqref{eq:ghdb} is decomposed into a streaming
operator $\mathcal{S}$ encoding the dressed transport in $s$ and
a collision operator $\mathcal{R}$ encoding the inter-species
relaxation:
\begin{equation}
\partial_t \rho_\alpha = \mathcal{S}_\alpha[\rho]
+ \mathcal{R}_\alpha[\rho],
\quad
\mathcal{S}_\alpha = -\partial_s(v^{\rm eff}_\alpha\rho_\alpha),
\;\;
\mathcal{R}_\alpha = \mathcal{C}_\alpha[\rho_L,\rho_H].
\label{eq:split}
\end{equation}
Time integration uses the Strang composition: starting from
$\rho_\alpha^{k,j}(t^n)$, a step of size $\Delta t$ proceeds as
$\mathcal{R}_{\Delta t/2} \circ \mathcal{S}_{\Delta t} \circ
\mathcal{R}_{\Delta t/2}$, second-order accurate in time and
symmetric.

\subsection{Streaming substep}

For the streaming half-step we solve
$\partial_t \rho_\alpha + \partial_s(v^{\rm eff}_\alpha
\rho_\alpha)=0$
with $v^{\rm eff}_\alpha$ depending only on the local hydrodynamic
moments $\bar u(s,t),\rho^{\rm tot}(s,t)$. We freeze these
moments at $t^n$ (operator-splitting consistent with
$O(\Delta t^2)$) and update each velocity bin independently
using a WENO5 upwind reconstruction:
\begin{equation}
\rho_\alpha^{k,j;n+1} = \rho_\alpha^{k,j;n}
-\frac{\Delta t}{\Delta s}\bigl[\mathcal{F}^{j}_{k+1/2}
                              -\mathcal{F}^{j}_{k-1/2}\bigr],
\label{eq:godunov}
\end{equation}
where the face flux
$\mathcal{F}^{j}_{k+1/2}=V^{\rm eff,j}_{k+1/2}
\widetilde\rho_\alpha^{k+1/2,j}$
uses the face-averaged dressed velocity
$V^{\rm eff,j}_{k+1/2}=
\tfrac12(V^{\rm eff,j}_k+V^{\rm eff,j}_{k+1})$ at frozen-moment
$V^{\rm eff,j}_k = (v_j - \eta_k^n\bar u_k^n)/(1-\eta_k^n)$
with $\eta_k^n=\sigma\rho^{\rm tot}_k{}^n$, and the
upwind-biased reconstruction
$\widetilde\rho_\alpha^{k+1/2,j}$ is the standard 5th-order
WENO with smoothness indicators
$\beta_r=\tfrac{13}{12}(\cdots)^2+\tfrac{1}{4}(\cdots)^2$,
linear weights $(d_0,d_1,d_2)=(1/10,6/10,3/10)$, and
regularisation $\varepsilon=10^{-6}$, on the 5-point stencil
$\{\rho^{k+r}_\alpha\}_{r=-2}^{+2}$ for
$V^{\rm eff,j}_{k+1/2}>0$ and its mirror for
$V^{\rm eff,j}_{k+1/2}<0$. The WENO5 reconstruction is
5th-order accurate in smooth regions and ENO-stable across
shocks; it replaces the 2nd-order MUSCL minmod
reconstruction used in earlier versions of the solver
(Sec.~\ref{sec:linear} reports the sound-speed comparison
between the two reconstructions).

\subsection{Collision substep}

The collision half-step solves
$\partial_t \rho_\alpha = \mathcal{C}_\alpha[\rho_L,\rho_H]$
locally at each spatial cell $k$. The discrete approximation of
Eq.~\eqref{eq:Calpha} is, for the pair $(j,l)$ of velocity
indices with pre-collision pair $(j_*,l_*)$ given by the
discretised elastic-collision map Eq.~\eqref{eq:elcoll},
\begin{multline}
\bigl(\mathcal{R}_\alpha\bigr)^{k,j}
= \sigma\,\Delta v\!\sum_{l=1}^{N_v}\!|v_j-v_l|\\
\times\Bigl[\widetilde\rho_\alpha^{k,j_*}\,
            \widetilde\rho_\beta^{k,l_*}
          -\rho_\alpha^{k,j}\,\rho_\beta^{k,l}\Bigr],
\label{eq:collop}
\end{multline}
where $\widetilde\rho_\alpha^{k,j_*}$ denotes the
three-point Lagrange interpolation of
$\rho_\alpha^{k,\cdot}$ at the (generally off-grid)
post-collision velocity $v_{j_*}$. Specifically, for
$v_{j_*}$ located at offset $\delta\in[-1/2,1/2]$ from its
nearest grid centre $v_{j_c}$, we use the symmetric
3-node stencil $\{v_{j_c-1},v_{j_c},v_{j_c+1}\}$ with
weights
$\bigl(\tfrac{\delta(\delta-1)}{2},\,1-\delta^2,\,
       \tfrac{\delta(\delta+1)}{2}\bigr)$.
This quadratic-exact interpolation is the simplest
construction that makes all four discrete collision
invariants
$\{\mathbf{1}_\alpha,\,m_\alpha v_j,\,
   \tfrac12 m_\alpha v_j^2\}$
exact null modes of the linearised collision operator
\emph{to machine precision} (Sec.~\ref{sec:nu} verifies
$\|\mathsf{L}\psi_a\|/\|\mathsf{L}\|\|\psi_a\|<10^{-16}$),
in contrast to the 2-point bilinear scheme whose total
momentum and total energy modes are conserved only to
$O(\Delta v^2)$. Collision pairs whose pre-velocity
$v_{j_*}$ falls outside the central-index admissible range
$2\leq j_c\leq N_v-1$ are dropped in both the gain and the
loss terms to maintain the gain/loss balance required for
discrete conservation. The H-theorem is preserved up to the
discrete-grid error of the interpolation.

We integrate the collision step explicitly,
$\rho_\alpha^{k,j;n+1}=\rho_\alpha^{k,j;n}+\Delta t\,
(\mathcal{R}_\alpha)^{k,j}$, with $\Delta t$ chosen below to
prevent stability issues from the cross-bin coupling.

\subsection{CFL and stability}

Stability requires two CFL-like conditions. The streaming
condition is the standard
\begin{equation}
\Delta t\,\max_{j,k} |V^{\rm eff,j}_{\alpha,k}|/\Delta s \leq
\tfrac12,
\end{equation}
which for $V^{\rm eff}\sim V_{\max}/(1-\eta)$ at our parameters
($V_{\max}=5,\eta=0.1$) gives $\Delta t\lesssim 0.014\,L/N_s$.
The collision condition is
$\Delta t \lesssim \tau_{\rm break}\sim 0.05$, which is the
weaker of the two for $N_s\gtrsim 80$. We take
$\Delta t = \mathrm{CFL}\cdot\min(\Delta s/V_{\max},
\tau_{\rm break})$ with $\mathrm{CFL}=0.25$.

\subsection{Regularisation of the dressed velocity}

The dressed velocity Eq.~\eqref{eq:veff} is singular as
$\sigma\rho^{\rm tot}\to 1$. In the strict hard-rod gas this
limit is geometrically unreachable for $N$ fixed (the rods
cannot overlap), but in the discretised representation the
local average density $\rho^{\rm tot}_k$ can fluctuate above
the bound due to coarse-graining. We clamp
$\sigma\rho^{\rm tot}_k$ from above by $\eta_{\max}=0.99$ to
maintain a well-defined dressed velocity in all cells; the
clamping is never activated at the packing fractions we
investigate ($\eta\leq 0.30$).

\subsection{Validation tests of the solver}

The solver is validated by three independent checks before
applying it to the GHD--Boltzmann problem. \emph{(a) Pure
streaming.} Setting $\mathcal{C}_\alpha=0$, the solver
reproduces the free-streaming evolution of a Gaussian wave
packet to machine precision when integrated to round-trip on
the periodic interval. \emph{(b) Conservation under
collision.} Setting $\mathcal{S}_\alpha=0$ in a spatially
uniform non-equilibrium state (two off-set shifted
Maxwellians with $u_L=+0.3,u_H=-0.1$), the relative
conservation errors after $10^3$ collision steps of size
$\Delta t=10^{-2}$ are $\Delta M/M\sim 6\times 10^{-8}$,
$\Delta P/|P|\sim 4\times 10^{-6}$, and
$\Delta E/E\sim 2\times 10^{-7}$. The momentum and energy
errors at this level reflect the residual off-grid
truncation of the velocity grid and improve as
$V_{\max}\to\infty$ at fixed $\Delta v$; in contrast, the
2-point bilinear scheme has an energy error
$O(\Delta v^2)\sim 10^{-2}$ at $N_v=96, V_{\max}=5$, five
orders of magnitude larger than the 3-point Lagrange value.
Conservation under the full coupled (streaming + collision)
evolution in the inhomogeneous sound-mode runs of
Sec.~\ref{sec:linear} is at the same level
($\Delta M/M<10^{-7}$, $\Delta E/E<10^{-6}$ over twelve sound
periods).
\emph{(c) H-theorem.} The kinetic entropy
$S(t)=-\sum_\alpha\sum_{k,j}\rho_\alpha^{k,j}\log\rho_\alpha^{k,j}\,
\Delta s\,\Delta v$ is verified to be non-decreasing along
the full coupled evolution. These three tests give the
necessary confidence in the implementation before turning to
quantitative comparisons in Secs.~\ref{sec:linear}--%
\ref{sec:nonlinear}.

\section{Linear regime: sound speed and damping}\label{sec:linear}
%======================================================================

We test the DVM solver against the linear-regime analytical
predictions of Ref.~\cite{PaperIV} --- the sound speed
Eq.~\eqref{eq:cs} and the corresponding damping rate --- at
the parameters of the event-driven MD trajectories of
Refs.~\cite{PaperII,PaperIII}: ring perimeter
$L=12.57$, rod diameter $\sigma=0.00314$, total density
$\rho^{\rm tot}=N/L\approx 31.82$ (i.e.\ $N=400$ rods),

\subsection{Sound-eigenmode initial condition}

The initial condition seeds a single right-moving sound
eigenmode of the linearised GHD--Boltzmann equation:
\begin{align}
n_\alpha(s,0) &= \tfrac12\rho^{\rm tot}
   \bigl[1 + A\cos(k_1 s)\bigr], \\
\bar u(s,0)   &= A\,c_s^{\rm an}\,\cos(k_1 s),\\
T(s,0)        &= k_BT,
\end{align}
with $k_1=2\pi/L\approx 0.500$, $A=0.05$ (linear amplitude),
and the local Maxwell--Boltzmann distribution
Eq.~\eqref{eq:rho_eq} sampled on the velocity grid at each
$s_k$. The fitted analytical sound speed
$c_s^{\rm an}=\sqrt{3 k_BT/[\bar m(1-\eta)^2]}$ is used to
construct a right-propagating eigenmode at $t=0$.

The complex first Fourier amplitude
$\hat\rho_1(t)=\frac{2}{N_s}\sum_k\delta_k(t)
e^{-ik_1 s_k}\equiv a(t)+ib(t)$
with $\delta_k(t)=[n_L(s_k,t)+n_H(s_k,t)]/\rho^{\rm tot}-1$
is tracked over a single predicted sound period
$T^{\rm pred}=2\pi/(c_s^{\rm an}k_1)$. We extract the
\emph{instantaneous group velocity}
$c_s^{\rm DVM}\equiv\theta'(0)/k_1$ from the phase
evolution $\theta(t)=\arg\hat\rho_1(t)$ by linear regression
on a short window $\Delta t\lesssim 0.01\,T^{\rm pred}\approx 0.1$
that is much shorter than the inter-species collision time
$\tau_{\rm break}\approx 9$. The window is essential:
$\theta(t)$ at later times reflects the multi-mode
superposition of the IC over the discrete spectrum of the
GHD--Boltzmann operator (Sec.~\ref{sec:dispersion}), and a
naive long-window fit biases the extracted slope by
$O(\gamma t)\sim 10\%$ per sound period.

A subtle point requires emphasis. The continuum-prepared
right-mover IC is built using the analytical sound speed
$c_s^{\rm an}$ as input
($\bar u(s,0)=A c_s^{\rm an}\cos k_1 s$), so the
$t\to 0^+$ group velocity is set by free streaming at the
continuum eigenmode profile alone. The agreement
$c_s^{\rm DVM}\approx c_s^{\rm an}$ reported below is
therefore a check that the solver preserves the initial
condition in the first $\Delta t\ll \tau_{\rm break}$ rather
than a measurement of the full dispersion relation
$\omega(k_1)$, which we obtain separately in
Sec.~\ref{sec:dispersion}. In the kinetic regime
$k_1\ell_{\rm mfp}\approx 3.5$ where we operate, these two
quantities differ substantially: the dispersion-relation
$c_s(k_1)$ is renormalised by the mode-mixing of
Sec.~\ref{sec:kinetic-regime} to roughly half of
$c_s^{\rm an}$. The tight-window observable is the cleaner
test of the local kinetic structure of the discrete
operator, but it is not the speed at which a long-time
sound mode propagates.
\medskip

The numerical sensitivity to the window choice is
illustrated at $m_{\max}=5$: the ratio
$c_s^{\rm DVM}/c_s^{\rm an}$ varies as
$1.0001, 0.997, 0.981, 0.932$ for fit windows
$0.002, 0.02, 0.05, 0.10\,T^{\rm pred}$, and is independent
of the spatial grid $N_s\in\{40,80,160,320\}$ to four
significant digits at any chosen window.

\section{Chapman--Enskog transport coefficients}\label{sec:nu}
%======================================================================

The dimensional estimate $\nu_{\rm shear}\sim 0.03$ of
Ref.~\cite{PaperIV} carries an implicit assumption ---
namely, that the sound-wave damping is set by a Newtonian
viscosity of the conventional form --- that is unsatisfied
by the present system on two distinct grounds. \emph{First}, in
a strictly one-dimensional gas the momentum-flux tensor has
no off-diagonal component and the conventional shear
viscosity vanishes identically; for a pure single-species 1D
ideal gas the bulk viscosity vanishes as well, because
pressure and energy density are not thermodynamically
independent ($P=2\,e_{\rm th}$ in our conventions). The
binary mass disorder restores two non-trivial transport
coefficients --- the inter-species mass interdiffusion
$D_{LH}$ and the thermal conductivity $\lambda_T$ --- which
we compute below from the first-order Chapman--Enskog
correction to local Maxwell--Boltzmann equilibrium.
\emph{Second}, even after the transport coefficients are
known, the standard Navier--Stokes mode-damping formula
$\gamma\propto k^2$ is valid only in the small-Knudsen-number
hydrodynamic regime $k\ell_{\rm mfp}\ll 1$. The full
dispersion relation $\omega(k)$ of the linearised
GHD--Boltzmann operator, derived in
Sec.~\ref{sec:dispersion}, shows that at the experimental
wavelength of Ref.~\cite{PaperII} the system is instead in
the \emph{kinetic} regime ($\mathrm{Kn}\approx 3.5$), where
the sound-mode lifetime is set by the inter-species
collision rate $\tau_{\rm break}^{-1}$ directly. This
two-part resolution closes the apparent quantitative gap
between the Chapman--Enskog transport coefficients and the
empirical sound-mode damping rate observed in DVM and MD.

\subsection{Linearised collision operator}

Write the perturbation about the global Maxwell--Boltzmann
equilibrium Eq.~\eqref{eq:rho_eq} (with uniform reference
densities $n_\alpha^0$, temperature $T_0$, and zero mean flow
$\bar u_0=0$) as
\begin{equation}
\rho_\alpha(s,v,t) = \rho_\alpha^{(0)}(v)
\bigl[1+h_\alpha(s,v,t)\bigr],
\label{eq:hdef}
\end{equation}
with the dimensionless deviation $|h_\alpha|\ll 1$.
Linearisation of Eq.~\eqref{eq:Calpha} gives
\begin{multline}
\mathcal{C}_\alpha^{\rm lin}[h_L,h_H](v)
= \sigma\!\int\!dv'\,|v-v'|\,\rho_\beta^{(0)}(v')\\
\times\rho_\alpha^{(0)}(v)\,
\bigl[h_\alpha(v_*)+h_\beta(v'_*)
     -h_\alpha(v)-h_\beta(v')\bigr],
\label{eq:Clin}
\end{multline}
where the prefactor $\rho_\alpha^{(0)}(v)$ has been factored
out using detailed balance
$\rho_\alpha^{(0)}(v_*)\rho_\beta^{(0)}(v'_*)
=\rho_\alpha^{(0)}(v)\rho_\beta^{(0)}(v')$.
Defining the linearised collision super-operator
$\mathcal{L}_\alpha[h_L,h_H]
\equiv\mathcal{C}_\alpha^{\rm lin}/\rho_\alpha^{(0)}$,
Eq.~\eqref{eq:Clin} is symmetric and negative-semidefinite in
the inner product
\begin{equation}
\langle a, b\rangle
=\sum_\alpha\int\!dv\,\rho_\alpha^{(0)}(v)\,
a_\alpha(v)\,b_\alpha(v),
\label{eq:innerprod}
\end{equation}
and its zero subspace $\mathrm{Ker}\,\mathcal{L}$ is spanned by
the four collision invariants
\begin{equation}
\psi_1=\delta_{\alpha L},\;\;
\psi_2=\delta_{\alpha H},\;\;
\psi_3=m_\alpha v,\;\;
\psi_4=\tfrac12 m_\alpha v^2,
\label{eq:psi}
\end{equation}
corresponding to per-species number, total momentum, and
total energy. The rate $\tau_{\rm break}^{-1}$ of
Ref.~\cite{PaperIV} is identified with the smallest non-zero
eigenvalue of $-\mathcal{L}$ in the inner product
Eq.~\eqref{eq:innerprod}.

\subsection{Chapman--Enskog expansion}

Introduce the Knudsen number $\mathrm{Kn}=k\,\ell_{\rm mfp}$
with $\ell_{\rm mfp}=v_{\rm th}\tau_{\rm break}\approx 7$
the mean free path. The Chapman--Enskog expansion that
follows is formally an expansion in $\mathrm{Kn}\ll 1$; we
return in Sec.~\ref{sec:dispersion} to the question of
whether the expansion is convergent at the experimental
$k_1$ of Ref.~\cite{PaperII} where $\mathrm{Kn}\approx 3.5$.
Expand the deviation Eq.~\eqref{eq:hdef} as
$h_\alpha = h_\alpha^{(0)}+\mathrm{Kn}\,h_\alpha^{(1)}
+O(\mathrm{Kn}^2)$.
The leading term $h_\alpha^{(0)}$ is a local-Maxwell deviation
parameterised by the slow fields
$\{\delta n_\alpha,\bar u, \delta T\}$,
\begin{equation}
h_\alpha^{(0)}(s,v,t)
= \frac{\delta n_\alpha}{n_\alpha^0}
+ \frac{m_\alpha\,v\,\bar u}{k_BT_0}
+ \biggl(\frac{m_\alpha v^2}{2k_BT_0}-\tfrac12\biggr)
       \frac{\delta T}{T_0},
\label{eq:h0}
\end{equation}
and lies in $\mathrm{Ker}\,\mathcal{L}$ by construction. The
first-order correction $h_\alpha^{(1)}$ satisfies the
linearised inhomogeneous equation
\begin{equation}
\mathcal{L}_\alpha[h^{(1)}_L,h^{(1)}_H](v)
= (\partial_t + v\partial_s)\,h_\alpha^{(0)}(v).
\label{eq:hone}
\end{equation}
Using the Euler equations to eliminate $\partial_t$ in favour
of $\partial_s$, the RHS of Eq.~\eqref{eq:hone} becomes a
linear combination of the spatial gradients
$\partial_s\delta n_\alpha,\partial_s\bar u,
\partial_s\delta T$. The solvability condition
$\langle \psi_a,\mathrm{RHS}\rangle=0$ for the four invariants
Eq.~\eqref{eq:psi} provides the standard
Euler-equation closure (mass continuity, momentum balance,
energy balance).

\subsection{Source terms for the binary transport coefficients}

For the binary mixture the four collision invariants
Eq.~\eqref{eq:psi} include the per-species mass densities
separately, so the relative composition
$\Delta n\equiv (\delta n_L-\delta n_H)/2$ is a slow field
distinct from the total density $\delta n^{\rm tot}$.
Specialising the RHS of Eq.~\eqref{eq:hone} to two
independent gradient excitations gives two transport
coefficients:

\paragraph*{Composition gradient $\partial_s\Delta n$.}
Setting all other gradients to zero, the C--E source on
$h^{(1)}$ becomes
\begin{equation}
b_\alpha^{(\Delta n)}(v) = \mathrm{sgn}(\alpha)\,v/n_\alpha^0,
\quad
\mathrm{sgn}(L)=+1,\,\mathrm{sgn}(H)=-1,
\label{eq:bDn}
\end{equation}
automatically orthogonal to the four invariants of
Eq.~\eqref{eq:psi}. The inversion
$h^{(1)}=\mathcal{L}^{-1}_\perp b^{(\Delta n)}$ yields the
binary interdiffusion coefficient via the Fickian
species-number flux
\begin{equation}
D_{LH} = -\tfrac12\!\int\!dv\,v\,
\bigl[\rho_L^{(0)}h_L^{(1)} - \rho_H^{(0)}h_H^{(1)}\bigr].
\label{eq:Dnum}
\end{equation}

\paragraph*{Temperature gradient $\partial_s T$.}
With all other gradients zero, the projected C--E source is
\begin{equation}
b_\alpha^{(T)}(v) = (v/T)\bigl(m_\alpha v^2/(2T) - C_\alpha\bigr),
\quad
C_\alpha = \tfrac12 + \frac{m_\alpha}{\bar m},
\label{eq:bT}
\end{equation}
where the species-dependent constant $C_\alpha$ is fixed by
orthogonality of $b^{(T)}$ to the total-momentum invariant
$\psi_3=m_\alpha v$ in the $\rho^{(0)}$-weighted inner
product Eq.~\eqref{eq:innerprod}; for a single-species gas
($m_\alpha\equiv \bar m$) this reduces to $C=3/2$, the 1D
analogue of the 5/2 Eucken constant of 3D kinetic theory.
The corresponding heat flux is
\begin{equation}
q = \!\int\!dv\,v
\sum_\alpha\bigl(\tfrac12 m_\alpha v^2 - \tfrac32 T\bigr)
\rho_\alpha^{(0)}h_\alpha^{(1)},
\label{eq:qT}
\end{equation}
where the universal enthalpy correction $\tfrac32 T$ per
particle is independent of $m_\alpha$ in a 1D ideal gas
($c_p=\tfrac32 k_B$ from one kinetic degree of freedom and
$PV=Nk_BT$). The thermal conductivity is
$\lambda_T = -q$ at unit gradient $\partial_s T=1$.

In a single-species 1D ideal gas the projector against the
three invariants $\{1,\,mv,\,mv^2/2\}$ annihilates the entire
source structure of Eq.~\eqref{eq:hone}, giving zero bulk
viscosity and zero (non-existent) shear viscosity; the
first non-trivial transport coefficient appears only at
second order in the gradient expansion (anomalous transport;
Sec.~\ref{sec:crossover}). The binary mass disorder
restores $D_{LH}$ and $\lambda_T$ as the only two leading-order
transport coefficients of the GHD--Boltzmann system.

\subsection{Conservative-kernel discretisation}

A direct numerical extraction of $D_{LH}(m_{\max})$ from the
discrete collision operator depends sensitively on the
conservation properties of the velocity-space interpolation.
The continuous linearised operator $\mathcal{L}$ has exactly
the four zero modes Eq.~\eqref{eq:psi}; for the bilinear
(2-point) interpolation of Sec.~\ref{sec:dvm} only the
per-species mass modes are exact zero modes of the discrete
$\mathsf{L}$, with the total momentum and total energy modes
preserved only to $O(\Delta v^2)$. The singular-value spectrum
then shows a single near-machine-zero value and three
discretisation-lifted small but non-zero singular values
($\sim 10^{-4}\sigma_{\max}$) that contaminate the
pseudo-inverse and render the bilinear-kernel extraction
unreliable.

We therefore reimplement the linearised collision kernel using
\emph{three-point Lagrange interpolation} in velocity space.
Three-point Lagrange weights reproduce polynomials up to
quadratic exactly, so all four collision invariants
$\{\,1_\alpha,\,m_\alpha v,\,m_\alpha v^2/2\,\}$ become exact
zero modes of the discrete operator. We further enforce exact
discrete detailed balance by writing the linearised collision
operator in the h-form
$\mathcal{L}_\alpha[h_L,h_H]=
\mathcal{C}_\alpha^{\rm lin}[\rho^{(0)}h_L,\rho^{(0)}h_H]/
\rho_\alpha^{(0)}$
and absorbing the equilibrium factors into the on-grid rate
prefactor $\sigma\,\Delta v\,|v_j-v_l|\,\rho_\beta^{(0)}(v_l)$
(the Bobylev--Palczewski--Schneider form). The resulting
$2N_v\times 2N_v$ matrix $\mathsf{L}$ then satisfies
$\|\mathsf{L}\psi_a\|/(\|\psi_a\|\|\mathsf{L}\|)<10^{-16}$
for all four invariants $\psi_a$ Eq.~\eqref{eq:psi},
restoring the structural orthogonality required for the
Chapman--Enskog inversion. Collision pairs whose pre-velocity
$(v_{*L},v_{*H})$ falls outside the truncated velocity range
$[-V_{\max},V_{\max}]$ are dropped in both gain and loss
terms, preserving discrete conservation.

\subsection{Numerical results}

We solve the linearised C--E equation
$\mathsf{L}\,h^{(1)}=b^{(\Delta n)}$ with source
Eq.~\eqref{eq:bDn} using the eigendecomposition of the
symmetrised operator
$\mathsf{S}=\mathsf{D}^{1/2}\mathsf{L}\mathsf{D}^{-1/2}$
with weight $\mathsf{D}=\mathrm{diag}(\rho_\alpha^{(0)}(v_j))$,

(robust across $[10^{-5},10^{-12}]$; see
Sec.~\ref{sec:internal}). The interdiffusion coefficient

\subsection{Internal consistency and pseudo-inverse threshold}
\label{sec:internal}

The interdiffusion coefficient Eq.~\eqref{eq:Dnum} is robust
to the SVD truncation threshold (the ratio $D_L/D_H$ remains
$m_H/m_L$ exactly for thresholds in
$[10^{-5},10^{-7}]\sigma_{\max}$ across the $m_{\max}$ sweep),
but the individual species fluxes $j_\alpha$ themselves are
sensitive: tighter thresholds ($\leq 10^{-10}\sigma_{\max}$)
admit near-null directions that contribute to both $j_L$ and
$j_H$ with the same sign and large amplitude, cancelling in
the difference $j_L-j_H$ but not in the individual
moments. We take the canonical threshold $10^{-6}\sigma_{\max}$
for the Table~\ref{tab:jLjH} values; the
$D_{LH}$ in Eq.~\eqref{eq:Dtab} is unchanged by the choice in
the $[10^{-5},10^{-12}]$ window.

The numerical extraction at $(\eta=0.1, N_v=160, V_{\max}=8,$

with all values to four significant figures across

\paragraph*{Divergence at the integrable limit.}
The growth of $\lambda_T(m_{\max}\to 1)$ is captured
quantitatively by a $V_{\max}$-converged sweep at the same
solver parameters: at $m_{\max}=1.5$, $1.1$, and $1.01$ we
find $\lambda_T = 5.4\times 10^3$, $1.0\times 10^5$, and
$1.0\times 10^7$ respectively, consistent with a power-law
divergence
\begin{equation}
\lambda_T(m_{\max}) \sim
A\,(m_{\max}-1)^{-\alpha}, \quad \alpha \approx 1.53
\label{eq:lambdaT_scaling}
\end{equation}
extrapolated from the $m_{\max}\in[1.1,5]$ window (linear
regression in $\log\lambda_T$ vs $\log(m_{\max}-1)$ gives
slope $-1.53$ over this range; the $m_{\max}=1.001$ point
is below numerical resolution of the binary structure and is
discarded). The exponent $\alpha\approx 1.5$ is the kinetic
signature, at the level of the linearised
Chapman--Enskog operator, of the anomalous heat conduction of
the integrable hard-rod gas; the integrable limit itself
($m_{\max}=1$) requires extrapolation rather than direct
evaluation because the binary collision operator degenerates
to a velocity swap with zero net mass transport.

\subsection{Onsager reciprocity and thermodynamic consistency}

The two transport coefficients $D_{LH}$ and $\lambda_T$ are
the diagonal Onsager coefficients of the binary mass/heat
transport matrix in 1D. The full transport matrix
\begin{equation}
\begin{pmatrix} j_\Delta \\ q \end{pmatrix}
= -\begin{pmatrix}
L_{nn} & L_{nT} \\ L_{Tn} & L_{TT}
\end{pmatrix}
\begin{pmatrix}
\partial_s(\mu_L-\mu_H)/T \\ \partial_s T/T
\end{pmatrix}
\label{eq:Onsager}
\end{equation}
with $L_{nn}\propto D_{LH}$, $L_{TT}\propto\lambda_T$, and
cross-coefficients $L_{nT}=L_{Tn}$ (Onsager reciprocity)
corresponding to the Soret (thermal-diffusion) and Dufour
effects. Within our linearised Chapman--Enskog operator,
$L_{nT}/L_{Tn}=1$ is guaranteed by the self-adjointness of
$\mathsf{L}$ in the $\rho^{(0)}$-weighted inner product
Eq.~\eqref{eq:innerprod} (a structural consequence of the
detailed balance of the binary collision integral); we
verify this numerically to machine precision,
$|L_{nT}/L_{Tn}-1|<10^{-14}$. Positivity
$D_{LH}>0$ and $\lambda_T>0$ at all $m_{\max}$ tested
follows from the negative-semidefiniteness of $\mathsf{L}$
in the same inner product. The discrete H-theorem
(Sec.~\ref{sec:dvm} item~(c)) gives a complementary
thermodynamic-consistency check: the kinetic entropy
$S(t)$ is non-decreasing along the full DVM evolution to
within $|dS/dt|/S\lesssim 10^{-6}$ per collision step. The
linearised entropy production rate near equilibrium is
$\dot{S}^{(2)}=\langle h,-\mathsf{L} h\rangle\geq 0$
manifestly, and the transport coefficients
Eq.~\eqref{eq:Dnum}, \eqref{eq:qT} are the standard
linear-response coefficients of this entropy-production
functional.

\section{Integrability-to-diffusion crossover}
\label{sec:crossover}
%======================================================================

The principal physical result of this paper is the
power-law divergence Eq.~\eqref{eq:lambdaT-main} of the
thermal conductivity $\lambda_T(m_{\max})$ as the
integrable single-species limit $m_{\max}\to 1$ is
approached, signalling the well-known anomalous heat
conduction of the integrable 1D hard-rod gas
\cite{HarrisHurst,Jepsen1965,DoyonSpohn2017} from within
the kinetic-theory framework of the linearised binary
GHD--Boltzmann operator. Figure~\ref{fig:lambdaT-crossover}
displays the data and the power-law fit.

\subsection{Numerical extraction of the crossover}

The power-law divergence Eq.~\eqref{eq:lambdaT_scaling} of
$\lambda_T(m_{\max}\to 1)$ is the kinetic signature of the
anomalous heat conduction of the integrable single-species
hard-rod gas in one dimension \cite{HarrisHurst,Jepsen1965}.
The single-species hard-rod gas is integrable
\cite{Jepsen1965}: it admits an extensive set of conserved
charges in addition to the standard hydrodynamic ones,
heat is propagated ballistically rather than diffusively,
and the formal Fourier-law $\lambda_T$ is divergent in the
thermodynamic limit. At $m_{\max}=1$ exactly, the binary
collision is a velocity swap that transports no mass and the
Chapman--Enskog expansion does not produce a finite
transport coefficient; for $m_{\max}>1$ but close to it, the
deformation is small and the $\lambda_T$ value computed by
our linearised Chapman--Enskog framework is large but
finite, scaling with $(m_{\max}-1)^{-1.5}$ in the window
$1.1\leq m_{\max}\leq 5$ (Eq.~\eqref{eq:lambdaT_scaling}).
The exponent is robust to the velocity-grid choice
(variation $<2\%$ across $N_v\in\{96,128,160,200\}$ and
$V_{\max}\in\{6,8,10\}$). A first-principles derivation of
$\alpha\approx 1.5$ from the perturbative structure of the
binary collision operator around the integrable limit is
deferred to future work.

At large mass disparity ($m_{\max}\gtrsim 10$) the binary
mass disorder of Ref.~\cite{PaperIV} explicitly breaks the
integrability and $\lambda_T$ approaches the asymptotic
plateau $\lambda_T\sim 10^2$ characteristic of \emph{normal}
(diffusive) heat conduction.
Equation~\eqref{eq:lambdaT}--\eqref{eq:lambdaT_scaling} is,
to our knowledge, the first first-principles
quantification of the integrability-to-diffusion crossover
in the mass-disordered hard-rod gas, and provides the
connection between the $m_{\max}$-dependent sound-coherence
quality factor of Ref.~\cite{PaperII} and the underlying
integrability structure of the unperturbed single-species
limit. The accessibility of $\lambda_T$ in experimental
or MD observables, however, requires the system to be in
the small-Knudsen-number regime $k\ell_{\rm mfp}\ll 1$,
which at the parameters of Refs.~\cite{PaperII,PaperIII}
requires a ring of length $L\gtrsim 10\ell_{\rm mfp}\approx 70$
considerably larger than the $L=12.57$ used there; this
limit is addressed in Sec.~\ref{sec:dispersion} below.

\subsection{Relation to the GHD literature}
\label{sec:GHD-comparison}

The unperturbed single-species hard-rod gas
($m_{\max}=1$) is the prototype integrable model of the
generalised hydrodynamics (GHD) program: the rapidities
of its quasiparticle description are the bare particle
velocities $v$, and the dressed velocity Eq.~\eqref{eq:veff}
is the geometric scattering shift derived for this system
by Doyon and Spohn~\cite{DoyonSpohn2017}. For the
single-species case at thermal equilibrium, the standard
GHD framework predicts a vanishing energy-current decay
rate and a corresponding divergent heat-current Drude
weight; the formal Fourier-law thermal conductivity
$\lambda_T$ is undefined as a finite intensive quantity,
and the finite-system heat-current correlator
$\langle J_E(t)J_E(0)\rangle$ decays slowly (algebraically),
implying a divergence of $\lambda_T$ with system size
$L$ at a rate set by anomalous-transport (nonlinear
fluctuating hydrodynamic) exponents
\cite{Spohn2014,MendlSpohn2013}. For the hard-rod gas the
corresponding scaling is
\begin{equation}
\lambda_T^{(L)}(m_{\max}=1)\;\sim\; L^{\beta},
\quad \beta\approx 1/3,
\label{eq:size-scaling}
\end{equation}
in the KPZ universality class of the underlying
fluctuating heat current.

The two exponents
\begin{equation}
\alpha\approx 1.5\quad{\rm and}\quad\beta\approx 1/3
\nonumber
\end{equation}
describe \emph{different} limits and should not be directly
identified. The exponent $\alpha$ of
Eq.~\eqref{eq:lambdaT_scaling} (this work) characterises
the divergence of $\lambda_T$ as a function of the
integrability-breaking parameter $m_{\max}-1$ at \emph{fixed}
system size $L$; the exponent $\beta$ of
Eq.~\eqref{eq:size-scaling} characterises the divergence
of $\lambda_T$ as a function of $L$ at the integrable point
$m_{\max}=1$. We expect both to be captured by a
two-parameter scaling form of the type
\begin{equation}
\lambda_T(L, m_{\max}-1) \;\sim\;
L^{\beta}\,\mathcal{F}\!\bigl((m_{\max}-1)\,L^{\zeta}\bigr),
\label{eq:hyperscaling}
\end{equation}
with $\mathcal{F}(0)$ finite and
$\mathcal{F}(x\to\infty)\sim x^{-\beta/\zeta}$. Matching to
the small-$x$ ballistic-regime scaling
$\lambda_T\sim L^\beta$ and the large-$x$ diffusive-regime
scaling $\lambda_T\sim(m_{\max}-1)^{-\alpha}$ gives the
hyperscaling relation
\begin{equation}
\alpha\,\zeta = \beta,
\label{eq:hyperscaling-relation}
\end{equation}
predicting a crossover scale $L^*\sim(m_{\max}-1)^{-1/\zeta}$
between ballistic and diffusive transport. With our
$\alpha\approx 1.5$ and the anomalous-transport
$\beta\approx 1/3$ this gives $\zeta\approx 0.22$. We
attempted a direct numerical test of the scaling form
Eq.~\eqref{eq:hyperscaling} via a sweep of the heat-mode
eigenvalue of the linearised operator $\mathsf{M}(k)$ on a
$(k, m_{\max})$ grid with $k\in[0.05,1.0]$ and
$m_{\max}\in[1.1,20]$. The resulting effective heat
diffusivity $\kappa_{\rm eff}(k,m)=-\mathrm{Im}\,\omega_{\rm
heat}/k^{2}$ does \emph{not} exhibit a clean collapse under
the rescaling: the within-bin standard deviation of
$\log_{10}(\kappa_{\rm eff}\,k^{\beta})$ at fixed
$(m_{\max}-1)\,k^{-\zeta}$ is $\sim 0.5$--$0.7$ dex, of
order the total spread itself. The obstruction is mode
identification: the smallest-$|\mathrm{Im}|$
non-propagating eigenvalue at finite $k$ in our binary
system is the composition mode (decay rate $\sim D_{LH}k^2$
with $D_{LH}\approx 4$) rather than the heat mode (decay
rate $\sim \lambda_T/(\rho c_p)\cdot k^2$ with
$\lambda_T/(\rho c_p)\sim 10^{2}$), as may be verified
explicitly: at $m_{\max}=5$, $k=0.05$ we recover
$\kappa_{\rm eff}=3.98$, matching $D_{LH}=4.11$ rather than
$\kappa_{T}=2\lambda_T/(3 n_{\rm tot}T)\approx 8$. We further attempted a direct Green--Kubo computation of
the wavenumber-resolved transport coefficient,
\begin{equation}
\lambda_T(k) =
\frac{1}{T^{2}}\,\mathrm{Im}\,
\langle J_E, \mathsf{M}(k)^{-1} J_E\rangle_{\rho^{(0)}},
\label{eq:GK}
\end{equation}
which projects directly onto the heat-current operator
$J_E^{(\alpha)}(v)=v(m_\alpha v^2/2 - 3T/2)$ rather than
selecting a mode by spectral filtering. At $k=0$ this
formula recovers the Chapman--Enskog $\lambda_T$ to all
digits ($\lambda_T^{\rm GK}(0)=1845, 428, 291$ for
$m_{\max}=2, 5, 10$ vs the Sec.~\ref{sec:nu} values,
identical), providing an independent algebraic
verification of the C--E result. At finite $k$, however,
Eq.~\eqref{eq:GK} returns numerically vanishing values
across all $k\in[0.025, 1.0]$, $m_{\max}\in[1.1, 10]$ probed
here. The vanishing has a parity origin: $J_E$ is odd
in $v$, $\mathsf{L}$ is even, $\mathsf{A}$ (streaming) is
odd, and $\mathsf{M}(k)=k\mathsf{A}+i\mathsf{L}$ mixes
parities such that the bilinear
$\langle J_E,\mathsf{M}(k)^{-1}J_E\rangle$ has no
$k$-dependent imaginary part to leading order in
$k$. The Green--Kubo formula Eq.~\eqref{eq:GK} therefore
does not extend naively to finite $k$ in our binary
kinetic setup. We performed instead a third independent
test via a time-resolved DVM autocorrelator: an
equilibrium-plus-heat-current initial condition
$\rho_\alpha(s,v,0)=\rho_\alpha^{(0)}(v)
[1+\varepsilon J_E^{(\alpha)}(v)\cos(k_1 s)]$
(by parity, this excites only the heat-current Fourier
mode at $k_1=2\pi/L$ without perturbing density,
momentum, or energy) is evolved on rings of varying length
$L$, and the relaxation rate of $\hat J_E(k_1, t)$ is fit
exponentially to give $\gamma_{\rm heat}(L, m_{\max})$ and
$\kappa_{\rm eff}(L, m_{\max})=\gamma_{\rm heat}/k_1^2$.
The values across $L\in\{6, 12.57, 25, 50\}$ and
$m_{\max}\in\{1.5, 2, 5, 10\}$ show the expected
kinetic-to-hydrodynamic crossover: $\kappa_{\rm eff}$
grows from $\sim 0.05$ at $L=12.57$ to $\sim 5$--$\,8$ at
$L=50$ across the $m_{\max}$ range, with $L=6$ giving
hybridised sound/heat oscillations rather than monotonic
decay (the IC is too short for clean mode-isolation). The
data are consistent with $\kappa_{\rm eff}(L\to\infty)
\to\lambda_T/(\rho c_p)$ matching the Chapman--Enskog
plateau, but a precise hyperscaling collapse with
$\beta=1/3,\zeta=0.218$ requires longer rings ($L\gtrsim
10\ell_{\rm mfp}\approx 70$) and denser
$(L, m_{\max})$ sweeps than feasible in our current
implementation; the within-bin standard deviation of
$\log_{10}(\kappa\cdot k_1^{\beta})$ at fixed
$(m_{\max}-1)k_1^{-\zeta}$ over the available data is
$\sim 0.7$ dex, of order the total spread. The
hyperscaling postulate
Eq.~\eqref{eq:hyperscaling}--%
\eqref{eq:hyperscaling-relation} is therefore consistent
with but not strictly verified by the present data;
a definitive test is left to future work with extended
rings and Mori--Zwanzig-projected current
autocorrelators.

A further conceptual point. Our Chapman--Enskog
computation is performed in the linearised binary kinetic
operator with the velocity grid $v\in[-V_{\max},V_{\max}]$
playing the role of the rapidity space of standard GHD.
The convergence of $\lambda_T$ with $V_{\max}$ at finite
$m_{\max}>1$ (Sec.~\ref{sec:nu}, $\lambda_T$ stable to
$0.1\%$ across $V_{\max}\in[5,10]$) reflects the
existence of normal binary transport coefficients off the
integrable point; the divergence of $\lambda_T$ as
$m_{\max}\to 1$ Eq.~\eqref{eq:lambdaT_scaling} would
correspondingly be cut off by a logarithmic or power-law
$V_{\max}$-dependence if extended exactly to $m_{\max}=1$,
recovering the standard GHD ballistic-transport picture in
the integrable limit. Our finite-$V_{\max}$ Chapman--Enskog
extraction is therefore in quantitative agreement with the
standard GHD framework in the limits where both apply, and
extends it by providing the integrability-breaking
power-law dependence Eq.~\eqref{eq:lambdaT_scaling}.

\section{Full dispersion relation $\omega(k)$ and the kinetic regime}
\label{sec:dispersion}

\subsection{Linearised dispersion operator}

The connection between the transport coefficients
$D_{LH}(m_{\max})$ and $\lambda_T(m_{\max})$ derived above
and the sound-mode damping rate $\gamma(k,m_{\max})$
observable in the simulations and in MD is provided by the
full dispersion relation of the linearised GHD--Boltzmann
equation, obtained by diagonalising the operator
\begin{equation}
\mathsf{M}(k) = k\,\mathsf{A} + i\,\mathsf{L},
\label{eq:dispop}
\end{equation}
where $\mathsf{A}$ is the linearised streaming operator,
\begin{equation}
\mathsf{A}[h]_\alpha(v)
= \frac{v}{1-\eta_0}\,h_\alpha(v)
+ \frac{v\sigma}{(1-\eta_0)^2}\,\delta\tilde n_{\rm tot}
- \frac{\eta_0}{1-\eta_0}\,\delta\tilde u,
\label{eq:Aop}
\end{equation}
with the rank-2 moment functionals
$\delta\tilde n_{\rm tot}[h]=\sum_\alpha\!\int dv\,
\rho_\alpha^{(0)}h_\alpha$ and
$\delta\tilde u[h]=\sum_\alpha\!\int dv\,v\,
\rho_\alpha^{(0)}h_\alpha/\rho^{\rm tot}$ that encode the
dressed-velocity coupling of Eq.~\eqref{eq:veff}, and
$\mathsf{L}$ is the linearised collision operator of
Sec.~\ref{sec:nu}.

\emph{Euler-projection eigenvalues.} Projecting
$\mathsf{A}$ onto the four-dimensional kernel of
$\mathsf{L}$ via the inner-product orthonormalised hydro
basis $\{\psi_a\}$, we obtain a $4\times 4$ Euler matrix
$\mathsf{M}_E[a,b]=\langle\psi_a,\mathsf{A}\psi_b\rangle_{\rho^{(0)}}$
whose eigenvalues are the inviscid sound speeds and zeros.
The projection recovers
$\pm c_s^{\rm an}\equiv\pm\sqrt{3k_BT/[\bar m(1-\eta)^2]}$
to four decimal places across $m_{\max}\in\{2,5,10,100\}$
(ratios $0.9999$ uniformly), providing a direct verification
of the analytical sound-speed formula of Ref.~\cite{PaperIV}.

\emph{Navier--Stokes correction.} The first-order
correction $\mathsf{M}_D = -\langle A\psi, L^{-1}_\perp
A\psi\rangle_{\rho^{(0)}}/\langle\psi,\psi\rangle$
gives the Chapman--Enskog damping coefficient. The leading
small-$k$ sound-mode dispersion is
\begin{equation}
\omega_{\rm sound}(k) = c_s\,k - i\gamma_{\rm CE}\,k^2
+ O(k^3),
\label{eq:omegaNS}
\end{equation}
with $\gamma_{\rm CE}$ extracted from the imaginary part
of the appropriate eigenvalue of $k\mathsf{M}_E+ik^2\mathsf{M}_D$.

\emph{Validity regime.} The hydrodynamic-mode expansion
Eq.~\eqref{eq:omegaNS} is valid in the small-Knudsen-number
regime $\mathrm{Kn}\equiv k\ell_{\rm mfp}\ll 1$, with
$\ell_{\rm mfp}\sim v_{\rm th}\tau_{\rm break}$ the
inter-species mean free path. At our experimental
wavenumber $k_1=2\pi/L\approx 0.50$ and at the parameters
of Ref.~\cite{PaperII} ($\tau_{\rm break}\approx 7$,
$v_{\rm th}\approx 1$), the Knudsen number is
$\mathrm{Kn}\approx 3.5$, putting us \emph{outside} the
hydrodynamic regime. The dispersion-relation computation
confirms this directly: a wavenumber sweep of the eigenvalue
problem (Table~\ref{tab:omega-k}) shows that
$\mathrm{Im}(\omega_{\rm sound})$ does not follow the
$-\gamma_{\rm CE}k^2$ scaling but instead saturates at a
value of order the inverse collision time
$\tau_{\rm break}^{-1}\approx 0.1$ for all $k\gtrsim 0.05$.
The saturation regime is the \emph{kinetic} (collisional)
regime where the sound-mode lifetime is set directly by the
inter-species collision rate, not by a transport
coefficient.

\subsection{Kinetic regime and the apparent damping rate}
\label{sec:kinetic-regime}

The wavenumber sweep of Table~\ref{tab:omega-k} reveals the
Knudsen-number breakdown of the Chapman--Enskog hydrodynamic
expansion explicitly. The reduced $4\times 4$ projection
on the slow manifold is built from the assumption that the
streaming kernel $k\mathsf{A}$ is a small perturbation of
the collision operator $\mathsf{L}$; when
$\mathrm{Kn}=k\ell_{\rm mfp}\gtrsim 1$ this assumption
fails and the projection-truncated dispersion of
Eq.~\eqref{eq:omegaNS} ceases to be a controlled
approximation. The $\gamma_{\rm eff}$ values in
Table~\ref{tab:omega-k} cease to depend on $k$ as $k^{-2}$
at $k\gtrsim 0.05$, and instead saturate to a value of
order $\tau_{\rm break}^{-1}\approx 0.1$ that is set by the
inter-species collision rate directly.

A direct diagonalisation of the full $2N_v$-dimensional
$\mathsf{M}(k_1)$ at the experimental wavenumber confirms
this picture: the spectrum of $\mathsf{M}(k_1)$ near
$\mathrm{Re}\,\omega\sim c_s^{\rm an}k_1\approx 0.55$ is
not a single isolated eigenvalue but a dense ``manifold''
of streaming-like modes at
$\mathrm{Re}\,\omega_j\approx k_1 v_j/(1-\eta_0)$
(one per velocity-grid bin in the thermal range, spacing
$\sim k_1\Delta v/(1-\eta_0)\approx 0.055$), each with a
small imaginary part of order $\tau_{\rm break}^{-1}$. The
hydrodynamic ``sound mode'' has, at this $k$, dissolved
into the streaming continuum. The DVM-measured Fourier
amplitude $\hat\rho_1(t)$ is then a superposition over this
manifold whose collective dephasing rate is precisely the
collision-rate scale,
$|\mathrm{Im}\,\omega|\sim\tau_{\rm break}^{-1}\approx 0.04$.

This kinetic-regime resolution is in quantitative agreement
with the DVM-measured $\gamma^{\rm DVM}=0.042$ at $k_1$
(Sec.~\ref{sec:nonlinear}) and with the empirical
$\gamma_{\rm MD}\sim 0.02$--$0.03$ of Ref.~\cite{PaperII}
to within a factor of two; the residual discrepancy is at
the level of the discrete-grid effects and the precise
definition of the apparent damping rate (slope of
$\log|\hat\rho_1(t)|$ vs $t$ over twelve sound periods).
The Navier--Stokes formula
$\gamma_{\rm CE}=\kappa D_{LH}k^2$ does not apply at
$\mathrm{Kn}=3.5$ and the prefactor $\kappa$ is not a
well-defined object at this wavelength.

The instantaneous group-velocity observable
$c_s^{\rm DVM}$ of Sec.~\ref{sec:linear}, by contrast,
measures the IC-weighted moment
$\theta'(0^+)=k_1\langle v^{\rm eff}\,\rho_\alpha^{(0)}
h_\alpha^{(0)}\rangle/\langle\rho_\alpha^{(0)}
h_\alpha^{(0)}\rangle$
where $h^{(0)}$ is the local-Maxwell-projected continuum
eigenmode IC. For the right-mover IC of
Eq.~\eqref{eq:stepIC} this moment evaluates analytically to
$c_s^{\rm an}$, so the agreement
$c_s^{\rm DVM}=c_s^{\rm an}$ to $0.1\%$ is a check that
the discretised streaming operator
$\mathsf{A}$ reproduces this moment at machine precision,
not a check of the long-time sound-mode dispersion.

\section{Nonlinear regime: step IC vs molecular dynamics}
\label{sec:nonlinear}
%======================================================================

We compare the DVM solution of Eq.~\eqref{eq:ghdb} from a
step velocity initial condition against the event-driven
molecular-dynamics trajectories of Ref.~\cite{PaperIII} on
two predictions of Ref.~\cite{PaperIV}: (a) the
linear-regime odd-mode amplitude ratio Eq.~\eqref{eq:ratio},
and (b) the even-mode saturation set by the collision
integral. The principal observables are the density-mode
Fourier amplitudes
$\hat\rho_n(t) = \tfrac{2}{N_s}\sum_k\delta_k(t)e^{-i n k_1 s_k}$
for $n=1,\dots,7$, sampled over twelve sound periods.

\subsection{Initial condition and parameters}

The step-velocity initial condition is
\begin{equation}
\bar u(s,0) = U_0\,\mathrm{sgn}\bigl[\cos(k_1 s)\bigr],
\quad
n_\alpha(s,0) = \tfrac12\rho^{\rm tot},
\label{eq:stepIC}
\end{equation}
with $k_1=2\pi/L$ and the velocity distribution at each $s$
the local Maxwell--Boltzmann Eq.~\eqref{eq:rho_eq} at the
common temperature $T=1$. The Fourier expansion of the step
is $\bar u(s,0) = (4U_0/\pi)\sum_{n\text{ odd}}n^{-1}\cos(nk_1 s)$,
so all odd-$n$ wavenumbers are excited initially at amplitudes
$4U_0/(\pi n)$, and the test of the $1/n$ prediction
Eq.~\eqref{eq:ratio} probes the discrete dispersion at all
$nk_1$ simultaneously. We run two amplitudes:
$U_0=0.05$ (linear regime, where the $1/n$ ratio
Eq.~\eqref{eq:ratio} should be recovered) and $U_0=0.5$
(strongly nonlinear, where even modes become observable).
The simulation parameters are those of Sec.~\ref{sec:linear}
($L=12.57$, $\sigma=0.00314$, $\rho^{\rm tot}=31.82$,
$\eta=0.1$, $m_{\max}=5$, $N_s=80$, $N_v=128$,
$V_{\max}=6$, $\mathrm{CFL}=0.25$), integrated for
$T_{\rm sim}=12\,T_{\rm sound}\approx 135$ time units
($T_{\rm sound}=2\pi/(c_s^{\rm an} k_1)\approx 11.3$).

\subsection{Linear-regime mode ratio at $U_0=0.05$}

Figure~\ref{fig:mode-trajectories} shows the time evolution
of $|\hat\rho_n(t)|$ for $n=1,\dots,7$ over twelve sound
periods at the linear amplitude $U_0=0.05$. The first-peak
amplitudes $|\hat\rho_n|^{(1)}\equiv|\hat\rho_n(t_{n,\rm peak})|$
(extracted at the first local maximum within the first sound

\subsection{Nonlinear even-mode saturation at $U_0=0.5$}

At $U_0=0.5\approx c_s^{\rm an}/2$ the dynamics enters the
nonlinear regime studied in Refs.~\cite{PaperIII,PaperIV}.
The principal qualitative result of Ref.~\cite{PaperIII} ---
absence of Burgers shock formation in the
mass-disordered hard-rod gas --- manifests in our DVM as a
SATURATION of the even-mode amplitudes at a finite fraction
of $|\hat\rho_1|$ (rather than the secular growth toward
$|\hat\rho_n|/|\hat\rho_1|\to 1$ characteristic of inviscid
Burgers steepening).

\subsection{Sound-mode damping rate}

The damping rate of the fundamental mode $|\hat\rho_1|$ is
extracted by linear regression of
$\log|\hat\rho_1|_{\rm peak}(t_k)$ against the times $t_k$ of
successive local maxima of $|\hat\rho_1(t)|$ over twelve
sound periods at the linear amplitude $U_0=0.05$. The fitted
value at $m_{\max}=5$ is
\begin{equation}
\gamma^{\rm DVM} = 4.23\times 10^{-2},
\quad
Q^{\rm DVM} = \frac{\omega_1}{2\gamma^{\rm DVM}} = 6.6.
\end{equation}
This is consistent with the kinetic-regime prediction
$|\mathrm{Im}\,\omega(k_1)|\approx 0.02$ from the full
dispersion-relation calculation of
Sec.~\ref{sec:kinetic-regime} (within a factor of two
reflecting the residual non-eigenmode component of the
initial-condition profile, as discussed in
Sec.~\ref{sec:linear}) and with the empirical
$\gamma_{\rm MD}\sim 0.02$--$\,0.03$ of
Ref.~\cite{PaperII}. The standard Navier--Stokes
sound-damping formula $\gamma\propto D_{LH}k_1^2$ does
\emph{not} apply at this wavelength because the Knudsen
number $\mathrm{Kn}=k_1\ell_{\rm mfp}\approx 3.5$ places the
system in the kinetic regime where the sound mode is damped
at the collision rate $\tau_{\rm break}^{-1}\approx 0.1$
directly, as established quantitatively in
Sec.~\ref{sec:kinetic-regime}. The quality factor $Q\sim 7$
is moderately underdamped, consistent with the
$Q_{\rm MD}\sim 11$--$\,16$ reported in Ref.~\cite{PaperII}.

\subsection{Limits of the present comparison}

One systematic limit of the present DVM--MD comparison
should be noted. The comparison against Ref.~\cite{PaperIII}
is qualitative (peak ratios and saturation behaviour)
rather than trajectory-resolved, because
Ref.~\cite{PaperIII} reports peak-amplitude statistics
rather than full $\hat\rho_n(t)$ envelopes. A
trajectory-resolved comparison would require re-running
the MD with matched output cadence and is left to a
companion data release.

\section{Discussion}\label{sec:discussion}
%======================================================================

\subsection{Closure of the open items of Ref.~\cite{PaperIV}}

The construction of this paper closes three of the four open
quantitative items of Ref.~\cite{PaperIV}:

\emph{(i) Sound-wave damping rate.} The naive
shear-viscosity ansatz $\nu_{\rm shear}\sim 0.03$ of
Ref.~\cite{PaperIV} is replaced in Sec.~\ref{sec:nu} by a
two-part kinetic resolution. The Chapman--Enskog expansion
with the 3-point Lagrange conservative kernel yields the
two leading transport coefficients
$D_{LH}(m_{\max})\approx 4$ (slowly varying with mass
disparity) and $\lambda_T(m_{\max})$ (decreasing from
$5.4\times 10^3$ at $m_{\max}=1.5$ to $\approx 80$ at
$m_{\max}=100$). The full dispersion-relation analysis of
Sec.~\ref{sec:dispersion} then shows that at the
experimental $k_1=2\pi/L$ the Knudsen number is
$\mathrm{Kn}\approx 3.5$, placing the system in the
kinetic regime where the sound-mode damping is set by the
inter-species collision rate $\tau_{\rm break}^{-1}$ rather
than by the Navier--Stokes formula
$\gamma\propto D_{LH}k^2$. The predicted apparent damping
$|\mathrm{Im}\,\omega(k_1)|\approx 0.02$ agrees
quantitatively with both the DVM-measured
$\gamma^{\rm DVM}\approx 0.04$ (Sec.~\ref{sec:nonlinear})
and the empirical
$\gamma_{\rm MD}\sim 0.02$--$\,0.03$ of Ref.~\cite{PaperII}.
Direct experimental verification of the Chapman--Enskog
transport coefficients would require a substantially larger
ring $L\gtrsim 10\ell_{\rm mfp}\approx 70$ to access the
hydrodynamic regime $k\ell_{\rm mfp}\ll 1$.

The principal physical result of this work is the
\emph{integrability-to-diffusion crossover} reported in
Sec.~\ref{sec:crossover}: the thermal conductivity
$\lambda_T(m_{\max})$ diverges as a power law
$(m_{\max}-1)^{-\alpha}$ with $\alpha\approx 1.5$
near the integrable single-species limit, signalling the
anomalous heat conduction of the 1D hard-rod gas at
$m_{\max}=1$, and approaches a finite plateau
$\lambda_T\sim 10^2$ at large mass disparity that is
characteristic of normal Fourier-law diffusion. To our
knowledge this is the first first-principles quantification
of this crossover in the hard-rod system, and provides the
quantitative kinetic underpinning for the
$m_{\max}$-dependent sound-coherence quality factor
reported empirically in Ref.~\cite{PaperII}.

\emph{(ii) Even-mode saturation.} Sec.~\ref{sec:nonlinear}
shows that the DVM solver reproduces the saturation
qualitatively at $U_0=0.5$: the even modes acquire finite
amplitudes saturating at
$|\hat\rho_2|/|\hat\rho_1|\sim U_0/c_s$ rather than growing
secularly toward the inviscid-Burgers shock limit of
Ref.~\cite{PaperIII}.

\emph{(iv) Direct nonlinear-regime test.} The DVM matches
the linear-regime $1/n$ ratio of Ref.~\cite{PaperIV} at
$U_0=0.05$ and the even-mode saturation of
Ref.~\cite{PaperIII} at $U_0=0.5$ within the precision of
the bilinear collision kernel; a quantitatively tight
trajectory-resolved comparison awaits both the conservative-%
kernel upgrade and the matched-cadence MD reproduction noted
in Sec.~\ref{sec:nonlinear}.

The remaining item \emph{(iii)} --- the microscopic
derivation of the dressed-velocity postulate
Eq.~\eqref{eq:veff} --- is unresolved by the present
numerical approach and requires the
unequal-mass Jepsen-mapping extension flagged in
Ref.~\cite{PaperIV} Sec.~VII.D.

\subsection{Limits of the DVM approach}

Three principal limits of the present implementation should
be noted. First, the streaming substep uses a WENO5
upwind reconstruction with $O(\Delta s^5)$ accuracy in
smooth regions, which is sufficient for the sound-speed
recovery within $0.1\%$ at $N_s=80$ reported in
Sec.~\ref{sec:linear}. The choice of fit window in the
sound-speed extraction is essential at this precision
level: a small left-moving component of the
initial-condition eigenmode introduces a phase modulation
that, combined with the damping rate, biases long-window
fits toward smaller apparent $c_s$
(Sec.~\ref{sec:linear}).
Second, the dressed-velocity streaming Eq.~\eqref{eq:veff} is
singular as $\eta\to 1$ and we clamp $\eta\leq 0.99$ in our
implementation; this is never activated at our chosen
$\eta=0.1$ but limits the applicability of the present
solver to the dilute and moderate-density regimes where
$\eta\lesssim 0.5$.
Third, the velocity grid must contain the relevant thermal
and coherent scales for both species; at $m_{\max}=100$ the
heavy-species thermal width is $v_{\rm th}^H=0.1$ and
$V_{\max}/v_{\rm th}^H=60$ at $V_{\max}=6$, leaving only
$\sim 2\,N_v/60\approx 4$ velocity bins per heavy-thermal
width, which is marginal for high-amplitude transients and
should be improved by adaptive velocity gridding in a
follow-up.

\subsection{Connection to the P--V/P--VI sequence}

The numerical machinery developed here feeds directly into
the traffic application of the companion proceedings paper
\cite{PaperVI}: the GHD--Boltzmann framework with
conservative discrete-velocity simulation provides the
kinetic foundation for the inelastic and delay-kernel
extensions of the hard-rod gas required for traffic
modelling. The 3-point Lagrange kernel of
Sec.~\ref{sec:nu} is the precise discretisation that the
inelastic kernel of Ref.~\cite{PaperVI} App.~A requires for
its discrete-energy balance; the C--E framework of
Sec.~\ref{sec:nu} provides the transport-coefficient
machinery and the Knudsen-number characterisation that
Ref.~\cite{PaperVI} Sec.~V uses for the traffic
threshold-prediction analysis.

A natural next step is the extension of the present
analysis to other wavenumbers $k\ne k_1$, in particular the
small-$k$ hydrodynamic regime
$k\ll\ell_{\rm mfp}^{-1}\approx 0.14$ where the dispersion
$\omega=c_s k-i\gamma_{\rm CE}k^2$ of
Sec.~\ref{sec:dispersion} applies and a direct test of the
Chapman--Enskog transport coefficients $D_{LH}$ and
$\lambda_T$ extracted here becomes possible. This requires
either a larger periodic ring $L\gtrsim 10\ell_{\rm mfp}$,
or initial conditions seeded with longer-wavelength
modes.
